1. What is CRM?

CRM (Customer Relationship Management) is a system used to manage a company’s interactions with customers and potential customers.

It helps businesses to:

Store customer information
Track sales and communication
Improve customer service
Manage leads and opportunities
Increase business growth

Salesforce is one of the most popular CRM platforms.

2. Why Companies Use Salesforce

Companies use Salesforce because it helps them manage business processes efficiently from the cloud.

Main Reasons:
Customer Management – Stores customer and sales data in one place
Sales Tracking – Tracks leads, opportunities, and deals
Better Customer Service – Provides faster support to customers
Cloud Access – Can be accessed from anywhere
Automation – Automates repetitive tasks and workflows
Reports & Dashboards – Helps analyze business performance
Customization – Businesses can customize apps and objects based on their needs

Salesforce also supports marketing, service, analytics, and mobile access.

3. Explain the Following
a) Account

An Account represents a company or organization you do business with.

Example:
TCS
Infosys
Amazon

It stores company-related information like:

Company name
Address
Phone number
Industry

b) Contact

A Contact is a person associated with an Account.

Example:

If the Account is Amazon, the Contact may be:

Raj Kumar – Sales Manager
Priya Sharma – HR Manager

It stores:

Name
Email
Phone number
Job title

c) Opportunity

An Opportunity represents a potential sales deal.

It helps track:

Deal value
Sales stage
Expected closing date
Example:

“Amazon purchasing 100 laptops worth ₹5,00,000”
